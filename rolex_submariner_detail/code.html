The Reference Among Divers’ Watches

Launched in 1953, the Submariner was the first divers’ wristwatch waterproof to a depth of 100 metres (330 feet). This was the second great breakthrough in the technical mastery of waterproofness, following the invention of the Oyster, the world’s first waterproof wristwatch, in 1926.

In watchmaking, the Submariner represented a turning point; it set the standard for divers’ watches. Today, the Submariner is equipped with calibre 3230 — or calibre 3235 for the Submariner Date — self-winding mechanical movements entirely developed and manufactured by Rolex.
